Recommended foods for 2-year-olds

Whole grains

Brown rice, oatmeal, whole wheat bread, and pasta. Great source of energy and fiber for growing toddlers.

EnergyFiber

Soft fruits

Banana, mango, papaya, melon, and ripe avocado. Easy to chew, rich in vitamins, and naturally sweet.

VitaminsHealthy fats

Vegetables

Sweet potato, carrots, squash, broccoli (steamed soft), and spinach. High in iron, calcium, and key vitamins.

IronCalcium

Lean proteins

Chicken, fish (boneless), eggs, and tofu. Essential for muscle growth and brain development.

ProteinBrain health

Dairy

Full-fat milk (up to 2 cups/day), yogurt, and soft cheese. Important for bone strength and calcium.

CalciumBone health

Legumes

Lentils, chickpeas, and mashed beans. Great plant-based protein and iron, especially important for toddlers.

ProteinIron
Foods to limit or avoid

Added sugar & salt

Avoid sugary drinks, sweets, and salty snacks. A toddler's kidneys and developing taste buds are very sensitive.

Choking hazards

Whole grapes, large chunks of hard food, whole nuts, popcorn, and hard raw vegetables. Always cut food small.

Quick recipe ideas

Sweet potato & chicken mash

Steam sweet potato and chicken breast until soft. Mash together with a small amount of butter. Easy to eat and very nutritious.

Oatmeal with banana

Cook rolled oats with milk. Top with mashed ripe banana. Great breakfast full of fiber and energy.

Egg & veggie scramble

Whisk 1 egg and cook with finely chopped spinach and soft tomato in olive oil. Serve with soft whole wheat toast fingers.

Lentil soup

Simmer red lentils with carrot, onion, and a pinch of turmeric until very soft. Blend smooth. High in iron and protein.

Yogurt & fruit bowl

Full-fat plain yogurt with mashed ripe mango or banana. No sugar needed — naturally sweet and packed with calcium.
